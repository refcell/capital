# Postmortems

This directory is for case studies where valuation, consensus expectations,
capital allocation, management credibility, or market structure broke in public
markets.

These are not full investment writeups. The goal is to build a library of
repeatable failure patterns: what the market appeared to underwrite, what was
already fragile before the event, what new evidence arrived, how the stock
reacted, and which pre-event signals should have been watched.

## Template

Each postmortem should start with the setup before the event. The event-day
move is the proof point; the useful work is identifying what had already been
fragile. A good postmortem should cover the company, ticker, date, release,
stock reaction, pre-event narrative, visible warning signs, breakpoint, market
math, why the reaction mattered, what a skeptical investor could have monitored
beforehand, and what evidence would confirm or invalidate the new view.

Primary filings and company releases should anchor the factual record. Market
data, analyst commentary, transcripts, and news should be used to explain
expectations and the change in perception. The useful output is pattern
recognition, not hindsight certainty.
