## 1. Thesis In One Page

### The Setup

Shift4 is more interesting than the label "payments processor" suggests. It is an integrated payments and commerce platform with strong positions in restaurants, hospitality, sports and entertainment, enterprise venues, and, after Global Blue, luxury retail tax-free shopping. The company is trying to own embedded payments in verticals where payment processing is tied to POS, software, operations, tax, FX, and customer experience.

The stock tells a more skeptical story. Q1 2026 gross revenue grew 32%, gross revenue less network fees, or GRLNF, grew 49%, adjusted EBITDA grew 39%, and 2026 guidance was reaffirmed. Yet the stock trades at a low multiple because the story is complicated: Global Blue, Smartpay, debt, preferred stock, GAAP versus adjusted earnings, founder transition, buybacks at leverage, and high short interest.

## 2. Company Story

### Origin And Evolution

Shift4 started as a payment gateway and grew into an integrated payments platform. It built strength in verticals where payments are embedded into merchant operations: restaurants, hotels, stadiums, arenas, resorts, airlines, specialty retail, and enterprise venues. SkyTab expanded the restaurant POS and commerce angle.

The company then became more global and more complicated through acquisitions. Global Blue added tax-free shopping and luxury retail exposure. Smartpay expanded Australia and New Zealand. Bambora added international capabilities. These assets can expand the TAM, but they also make organic growth and balance-sheet risk harder to read.

### Why The Company Matters

Shift4 matters because payments are most valuable when they sit inside merchant workflow. A restaurant, hotel, stadium, airline, or luxury retailer does not just need card acceptance. It needs POS, ordering, booking, loyalty, reporting, fraud, tax, FX, commerce, and settlement tools that fit the vertical. If Shift4 owns those workflows, the economics should be stickier than commodity acquiring.

## 3. Business Model

### How The Company Makes Money

Shift4 earns revenue from payment processing, gateway services, merchant acquiring, POS and commerce software, equipment, subscription services, and Global Blue's tax-free shopping business. Gross revenue includes large network-fee pass-throughs, so GRLNF is the cleaner economic revenue base.

| Revenue Stream | Description | Share / Size | Economics | Risk |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| Payments | Merchant acquiring, processing, gateway. | Largest revenue stream. | Volume, spread, and vertical attachment. | Take-rate pressure. |
| GRLNF | Gross revenue less network fees. | Q1 2026 $549M. | Better economic revenue metric. | Acquisition mix can distort growth. |
| SkyTab / restaurants | POS, ordering, commerce, payments. | Not separately disclosed. | Stickier vertical economics. | Toast, Square, Clover, Lightspeed. |
| Hospitality / venues | Hotels, resorts, stadiums, enterprise merchants. | Core vertical franchise. | Complex embedded workflows. | Cyclical demand and enterprise pricing. |
| Global Blue / TFS | Tax-free shopping and luxury retail. | Material after acquisition. | International travel and FX-linked economics. | Travel disruption, regulation, integration. |
| Subscription / other | Software, equipment, services. | Smaller but important. | Recurring and attachment economics. | Hardware subsidies and churn. |

### The Economic Engine

The engine is: win vertical merchants -> process more volume -> attach POS, software, tax-free shopping, FX, gateway, and commerce products -> grow GRLNF faster than volume -> convert GRLNF into adjusted EBITDA and FCF -> use cash to delever or repurchase shares.

The weak link is adjusted-to-owner earnings conversion. If adjusted EBITDA turns into cash and debt falls, the stock is cheap. If adjustments, interest, preferred dividends, and deal costs remain recurring, the cheap multiple is deserved.

## 10. Bull Case

If Shift4 is a vertical commerce platform rather than a commodity acquirer, the current valuation is too low. GRLNF can grow faster than volume as Shift4 attaches software, POS, FX, tax-free shopping, and commerce products. Global Blue can become a distribution wedge into luxury retail and international merchants. SkyTab can keep gaining restaurant share.

In that case, adjusted FCF grows from roughly $500M to $650M-$850M over several years, leverage falls, and the market rerates the stock from 7x current adjusted FCF toward 12x to 15x.

## 11. Bear Case

If Shift4 is a levered payments roll-up, the low multiple is deserved. Revenue and adjusted EBITDA can grow while GAAP earnings, owner earnings, and per-share value disappoint. Acquisitions add goodwill, intangibles, amortization, integration costs, and new operating risks. Debt and preferred stock absorb part of the upside.

The bear case does not need collapse. It only needs adjusted FCF to be lower quality than advertised, organic growth to slow, and investors to keep applying a low multiple.

## 12. Base Case

The base case is that Shift4 is messy but undervalued. The company hits 2026 guidance, Global Blue is imperfect but manageable, organic GRLNF grows high single to low double digits, and adjusted FCF gradually increases. Leverage does not disappear, but EBITDA growth lowers risk over time.

## 18. Conclusion

My stance is watch / high-upside, high-complexity. FOUR becomes more actionable if 2026 shows clean organic GRLNF growth, Global Blue integration progress, adjusted FCF conversion, and deleveraging. Until then, the stock is cheap enough to study but too complicated to underwrite casually.
