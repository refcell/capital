## Company Overview

### Founding & History

Zeta Global was founded in **2007** by **David A. Steinberg** and former Apple Inc. CEO **John Sculley** under the name "XL Marketing." The company was founded with the vision that data was going to change the way marketers engage with consumers to drive successful business outcomes.

**Key Milestones:**
- **2007**: Founded as XL Marketing with initial $3M investment from private investors
- **2014**: Renamed to Zeta Interactive
- **2015**: Raised $125M from Blackstone's GSO Capital Partners
- **2016**: Renamed to Zeta Global
- **2017**: Raised $140M, valuation reached $1.3 billion
- **2021**: IPO on NYSE (June 10, 2021) at $10/share
- **2024**: Reached $1.006 billion in revenue (38% YoY growth)
- **2025**: Acquired Marigold's enterprise software business
- **2026**: Partnered with OpenAI for Athena AI agent

**Headquarters:** New York City, NY

**Ticker:** NYSE: ZETA

## Business Model

### How Zeta Makes Money

Zeta Global operates as a provider of an **AI-powered marketing platform** that unifies data and marketing channels. The company's revenue comes from:

1. **Platform Subscriptions**: Enterprise customers pay for access to the Zeta Marketing Platform (ZMP)
2. **Managed Services**: Campaign execution and optimization services
3. **Data Services**: Customer data management and enrichment
4. **Media Activation**: Programmatic advertising and omnichannel activation

### Value Proposition

- **Unified Platform**: Combines CDP (Customer Data Platform), marketing cloud, and DSP (Demand-Side Platform) into one solution
- **Proprietary Data Assets**: Access to 200M+ US adult identity profiles with 2,500+ attributes each
- **AI-Powered Intelligence**: Predictive, generative, and agentic AI capabilities
- **Omnichannel Activation**: Seamless customer engagement across all paid and owned channels

### Revenue Model Characteristics

- **Recurring Revenue Model**: High retention with 114% net revenue retention rate
- **Enterprise Focus**: 44 of the Fortune 100 use the ZMP
- **Super-Scaled Customers**: 180 customers spending $1M+ annually (as of Q3 2025)

## Products & Technology

### Zeta Marketing Platform (ZMP)

The flagship product that unifies identity, intelligence, and omnichannel activation into a single platform.

**Core Components:**

#### 1. Zeta Data Cloud
The heart of the platform, offering:
- **Identity Graph**: 200M+ US adults (~90% of population)
- **2.5 billion signals** enriched with behavioral and transactional data
- **1 trillion signals collected per month**
- Combines PII (name, address, email) with digital identifiers (cookies, MAIDs, device IDs)

#### 2. Customer Data Platform (CDP)
- Ingests, cleanses, and unifies data from any source
- Creates continuously enriched, actionable customer profiles
- Real-time activation-ready profiles

#### 3. AI & Machine Learning
- **Predictive AI**: Forecasts customer behavior and outcomes
- **Generative AI**: Creates personalized content at scale
- **Agentic AI**: Autonomous workflow execution and optimization
- **130+ patents** protecting intellectual property

#### 4. Athena by Zeta (New in 2026)
Superintelligent AI agent for enterprise marketing, powered by OpenAI:
- **Insights**: Conversational analytics with instant trend and audience discovery
- **Advisor**: Goal-driven optimization with automated action execution
- Launching to all customers by end of Q1 2026

### Technical Differentiators

- **Full-Stack Solution**: Only platform with native CDP + Marketing Cloud + DSP integration
- **First-Party Data Focus**: 75%+ of identities from first-party sources
- **Privacy-First Architecture**: Works without third-party cookies
- **Pre-Built Industry Templates**: Tailored AI models for retail, financial services, media, telecom

## Risks & Controversies

### Culper Research Short Seller Report (November 2024)

On November 13, 2024, Culper Research published "**Zeta Global Holdings Corp (ZETA): Shams, Scams, and Spam**"

**Key Allegations:**
1. **Consent Farms**: Claimed Zeta collected data from "sham websites" that trick consumers
2. **Revenue Round-Tripping**: Alleged "two-way" contracts that flatter reported revenue
3. **Materiality Claims**: Said consent farms drove 56% of Adjusted EBITDA
4. **Regulatory Risk**: Drew parallels to Fluent Inc. FTC charges

**Stock Impact:** Shares fell 37% in one day (from $28.22 to $17.76)

**Zeta's Response:**
- Called report "riddled with misrepresentations, speculative conjecture, and categorically false statements"
- Stated Apptness and ArcaMax contribute <3% of YTD revenue and <1% of data assets
- Said "We do not operate so-called 'consent farms'"
- Pointed to unqualified audit opinion from Deloitte
- CEO and executives purchased $3M+ in shares following report

**Legal Aftermath:**
- Securities class action filed (Class Period: Feb 27 - Nov 13, 2024)
- Lead plaintiff deadline: January 21, 2025
- Investigation ongoing

[Sources](https://investors.zetaglobal.com/news/news-details/2024/Zeta-Global-Responds-to-Short-Seller-Report/default.aspx)

### Other Risks

1. **Customer Concentration**: Business concentrated in retail and financial services verticals
2. **Competition**: Adobe, Salesforce, Oracle have deeper pockets
3. **Acquisition Integration**: Marigold adds complexity
4. **Stock-Based Compensation**: Historically high dilution (though improving)
5. **Insider Selling**: Consistent selling despite recent buying
