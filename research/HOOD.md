# Robinhood Markets (HOOD) Investment Research

Date: 2026-05-14

Price snapshot: HOOD closed at $76.75 on 2026-05-13, the latest regular-market close before this report. Using StockAnalysis' 900.5 million shares outstanding, market capitalization was roughly $69.1 billion. Using Q1 2026 diluted weighted-average shares of 915.0 million gives a fully diluted equity value closer to $70.2 billion. Robinhood reported $5.012 billion of cash and cash equivalents at 2026-03-31, but I do not treat segregated customer cash, securities lending balances, customer payables, or other broker-dealer operating balances as excess corporate cash. On a practical basis, enterprise value is roughly $64 billion to $65 billion.

This is investment research, not personalized financial advice.

## Executive Summary

Robinhood is no longer just the 2021 meme-stock brokerage. It is a scaled retail financial platform with 27.6 million funded customers, $345 billion of Total Platform Assets at April 2026 month-end, $67 billion of trailing-twelve-month net deposits, 4.3 million Gold subscribers, a large margin book, a growing credit-card book, crypto exchange infrastructure through Bitstamp, RIA custody through TradePMR, advisory through Robinhood Strategies, and a fast-growing prediction-markets business.

The business is much better than the old caricature. FY2025 net revenue increased 52% to $4.47 billion, adjusted EBITDA increased 76% to $2.52 billion, net income was $1.88 billion, and Robinhood repurchased $653 million of stock. Q1 2026 still showed profitable growth despite a crypto slowdown: revenue increased 15% year over year to $1.07 billion, net income attributable to Robinhood was $350 million, adjusted EBITDA was $534 million, net deposits were $17.7 billion, and Gold subscribers grew 36% to 4.3 million.

The stock is harder. At roughly $76.75, HOOD trades around 15x trailing revenue, 37x trailing earnings, and roughly 25x to 27x my rough TTM adjusted EBITDA estimate depending on cash treatment. The market is not valuing Robinhood like a damaged broker. It is valuing it like a durable asset-gathering fintech compounder with meaningful embedded product optionality.

The central debate is therefore clean: is Robinhood becoming a structurally improving financial super-app, or is it still a high-beta trading, crypto, rates, and regulatory cycle with a better product roadmap?

The bull case is that Robinhood is acquiring sticky customer assets at high speed and then layering on products with attractive incremental economics: Gold, margin, options, event contracts, cash/deposits, retirement, advisory, card, crypto, private markets, and international expansion. The April 2026 data supports that version: Total Platform Assets were up 49% year over year, margin balances were up 114%, cash and deposits were up 59%, equity volumes were up 57%, options contracts were up 34%, and event contracts reached 3.2 billion in the month.

The bear case is that the earnings base is still not as durable as the multiple suggests. In FY2025, transaction-based revenue was 59% of net revenue. In Q1 2026, crypto revenue fell 47% year over year and event-contract revenue jumped from $3 million to $104 million. That is diversification, but it is also substitution from one volatile/regulatory-sensitive activity stream to another. PFOF, crypto rebates, rates, prediction markets, credit-card losses, customer behavior, liquidity-provider concentration, outages, and founder control all remain real issues.

My read is neutral on the stock and constructive on the business. Robinhood is a real platform with a stronger asset-gathering engine than skeptics expected. But at this price, the base case needs too much to go right. My DCF framework puts a conservative base case around $47 per share, a bear case around $18, and a bull case around $106. The stock can work if Robinhood compounds revenue at a mid-teens rate for a decade while maintaining very high after-SBC owner-earnings margins. It is not obviously cheap if revenue growth normalizes, rates fall, trading slows, or event-contract economics are regulated down before they mature.

## Company Overview

Robinhood Markets, Inc. operates a retail financial-services platform centered on brokerage, trading, cash, crypto, and financial-product engagement. The company reports in U.S. dollars and is listed on Nasdaq under ticker HOOD.

The product set now includes stocks, ETFs, ADRs, fractional shares, options, futures, event contracts, crypto trading/transfers/staking/lending where available, margin, securities lending, cash sweep, high-yield cash and deposits, retirement accounts, joint accounts, Robinhood Gold, credit cards, advisory portfolios, RIA custody through TradePMR, crypto exchange infrastructure through Bitstamp, private-market access through Robinhood Ventures, international brokerage/crypto expansion, and AI/social investing tools.

The company is explicitly trying to become the "Financial SuperApp." That phrase is promotional, but the strategic direction is real. Robinhood wants the first investing relationship, the active-trading relationship, the cash relationship, the crypto relationship, the card relationship, the retirement relationship, and eventually a broader wealth/advice relationship.

Definitions matter. Total Platform Assets include equities, options, cryptocurrency, futures and swaps including event contracts, customer cash net of receivables, assets managed by RIAs using TradePMR that are not custodied by Robinhood, and platform-enabled crypto lending assets. This is broader than the older "assets under custody" frame. Net deposits include customer cash deposits, transfers, dividends, interest, staking rewards, and promotional cash/assets, net of withdrawals, margin/lending interest, Gold fees, and transfers off platform.

## Business Model

Robinhood has three main revenue categories: transaction-based revenue, net interest revenue, and other revenue.

### Transaction-Based Revenue

Transaction-based revenue includes options, crypto, equities, event contracts, futures, and related transaction lines. For equities and options, economics include payment for order flow. For crypto, economics include transaction rebates, exchange fees, spreads, routing economics, and Bitstamp exchange activity.

In Q1 2026, transaction revenue was $623 million:

| Q1 2026 transaction revenue | Revenue |
|---|---:|
| Options | $260M |
| Crypto | $134M |
| Event contracts | $104M |
| Equities | $82M |
| Other transaction revenue | $43M |
| Total transaction-based revenue | $623M |

The mix change is important. Crypto revenue was down 47% year over year, but event-contract revenue increased from $3 million in Q1 2025 to $104 million in Q1 2026. Bulls call this proof that Robinhood can create new high-engagement products. Bears call it a reminder that the business still leans on products with volatile demand and unsettled regulatory treatment.

### Net Interest Revenue

Net interest revenue comes from margin loans, customer cash and deposits, segregated cash/securities/deposits, cash sweep spread, corporate cash and investments, securities lending, and credit-card net interest, net of related funding costs.

In Q1 2026, net interest revenue was $359 million, or 34% of total net revenue. Margin interest was the largest disclosed sub-line at $193 million. Credit-card net interest was $32 million. Securities lending net revenue fell sharply, showing that not all balance-based revenue is equally stable.

This is a better revenue stream than one-off trading spikes, but it is not risk-free. It depends on rates, customer cash behavior, margin balances, securities-lending demand, funding spreads, credit losses, and the competitive rate Robinhood pays users.

### Other Revenue

Other revenue includes Gold subscription fees, proxy revenues, digital-asset listing fees, selling concessions, advertising, ACATS transfer fees, and other items. Q1 2026 other revenue was $85 million, up 57% year over year. Gold subscription revenue was $50 million, up 32%.

Gold is one of the cleanest product-attach signals. A Gold subscriber can receive higher cash rates, margin benefits, research/tools, card access, deposit bonuses, and other bundled benefits. The economics depend on churn, incremental ARPU, reward/cash-rate cost, and whether Gold pulls customers into larger balances and higher-value products.

## Products and Platform Assets

### Brokerage and Active Trading

The brokerage platform remains the core front door. Robinhood offers equities, ETFs, ADRs, fractional shares, options, futures, index options, 24-hour market access, and event contracts. Options are economically important: FY2025 options revenue was $1.123 billion, up 48%, and Q1 2026 options revenue was $260 million, up 8%.

Active trading is a strength because it creates engagement, data, and monetization. It is also the source of the quality debate. Options, crypto, and event contracts can produce high revenue per active customer, but they expose Robinhood to trading cycles, customer outcomes, regulatory scrutiny, and reputational risk.

### Prediction Markets and Event Contracts

Prediction markets are the newest major upside vector. Q1 2026 event-contract revenue was $104 million, event contracts traded reached 8.8 billion, and April event contracts traded were 3.2 billion.

The bull case is that event contracts are a large new retail market adjacent to trading, sports, politics, economics, and current events. Robinhood has distribution, product design, and customer engagement advantages. The Rothera joint venture, created with Susquehanna International Group, could improve vertical integration and product control if execution and regulatory outcomes are favorable.

The bear case is that investors may be capitalizing a business before its legal footing and normalized economics are proven. Robinhood discloses state cease-and-desist letters, state actions alleging wagering/gaming-law violations, tribal litigation naming Kalshi/Robinhood/RHD with RICO claims, private gambling-loss recovery claims, and the possibility that adverse outcomes could prevent Robinhood from offering some or all event contracts in some jurisdictions or broadly.

### Crypto and Bitstamp

Crypto remains central to the Robinhood story but is no longer the only story. FY2025 crypto revenue was $901 million, up 44%. Q1 2026 crypto revenue was $134 million, down 47% year over year, even though total company revenue still grew.

Bitstamp gives Robinhood global crypto exchange infrastructure, institutional and retail crypto exchange capabilities, and international regulatory licenses. April 2026 crypto notional volume was $11.9 billion: $5.4 billion from the Robinhood app and $6.5 billion from Bitstamp. Robinhood app crypto notional volume was down 37% year over year in April, while total crypto notional volume was up because Bitstamp is now included.

The right conclusion is not that crypto no longer matters. It matters a lot. The better point is that the company has built enough other revenue lines that a weak app-crypto quarter is no longer automatically thesis-breaking.

### Gold, Banking, and Card

Gold subscribers reached 4.3 million in Q1 2026, up 36% year over year. Robinhood Gold Card crossed 800,000 funded customers.

Gold and card are strategically useful because they shift Robinhood from a trading app toward a daily financial relationship. But card economics need seasoning. Q1 2026 credit-card net interest revenue was $32 million, while the credit-card-related provision for credit losses rose 138% year over year to $31 million. The company said the increase was mainly due to higher purchased credit-card receivable balances. A premium card can look attractive during rapid growth and then show weaker economics as credit cohorts age.

### Retirement, Advisory, and RIA Custody

Robinhood Retirement AUC reached $26.5 billion at Q4 2025, up 102% year over year. Robinhood Strategies, the company's advisory product, had more than 285,000 funded customers and $1.6 billion of AUM to date in the Q1 2026 release. TradePMR adds RIA custody and expands the platform beyond self-directed retail accounts.

This is where the long-term bull case becomes more durable. If Robinhood can win retirement assets, recurring advisory fees, RIA assets, and family/household wealth relationships, then the business deserves a higher-quality asset-gathering multiple. If those products remain small relative to options, crypto, event contracts, and interest income, the quality discount remains.

### Private Markets and Trump Accounts

Robinhood is also pushing private-market access and government-linked account distribution. Robinhood Ventures Fund I was listed on the NYSE, and the company has discussed broader private-market access. Robinhood also disclosed expected 2026 costs related to Trump Accounts, and CNBC reported management's argument that the program could put Robinhood in front of a new generation of investors.

The bull read is low-CAC, long-duration customer acquisition. The bear read is that the monetization is distant, the cost base rises now, and government-linked distribution can create political and operational complexity.

## Industry and Competitive Position

Robinhood competes across multiple industries at once:

| Competitor type | Examples | Robinhood advantage | Robinhood disadvantage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Incumbent brokerages | Schwab, Fidelity, E-Trade/Morgan Stanley | Mobile-native UX, product velocity, younger customer base. | Trust, scale, advisory breadth, household assets, retirement penetration. |
| Active/pro brokers | Interactive Brokers, tastytrade | Consumer onboarding, simplicity, broader app engagement. | Execution depth, margin rates, global market access, pro tools. |
| Crypto exchanges | Coinbase, Kraken, Gemini | Brokerage distribution, broader financial app, lower-friction retail funnel. | Crypto-native depth, institutional custody, stablecoin/infrastructure economics. |
| Consumer fintechs | SoFi, Cash App, PayPal, Chime | Investing/trading identity, options/crypto/event engagement, asset-gathering momentum. | Banking/lending depth, direct deposit habits, mature payments networks. |
| Mobile brokers | Webull, Public, Moomoo | Brand scale, Gold, product breadth, net deposits. | Promotional competition, options pricing, switching ease. |
| Wealth/advice platforms | Schwab, Vanguard, Betterment, RIAs | Large young base and low-friction digital cross-sell. | Advice trust, tax/planning depth, advisor relationships. |
| Prediction-market platforms | Kalshi and others | Retail distribution and app engagement. | Legal uncertainty, market-structure dependency, not yet proven at scale. |

The moat is not a single product. Commission-free trading can be copied. High-yield cash can be copied. A premium card can be copied. The possible moat is the bundle: a large retail user base, a familiar consumer brand, self-clearing, brokerage infrastructure, crypto infrastructure, Gold bundling, retirement incentives, margin, event contracts, private markets, and product velocity in one app.

The risk is that the bundle is broad but not deep. Larger accounts may care more about execution quality, margin rates, tax tools, advisor access, borrow availability, cash rates, service quality, and trust. Robinhood can dominate the first-account and active-mobile segments while still losing the largest household balances to Schwab, Fidelity, Vanguard, Interactive Brokers, Coinbase, or RIAs.

## Management, Governance, and Capital Allocation

Vlad Tenev is chair and CEO. Baiju Bhatt remains a founder/director. Shiv Verma became CFO effective February 2026 after Jason Warnick's retirement transition. On May 8, 2026, Robinhood filed an 8-K stating that CTO Jeffrey Pinner separated from the company effective May 7, 2026.

Founder control remains important. The 2026 proxy states that Class B shares carry ten votes per share, are held exclusively by the co-founders and related entities, and that parties to the Founders' Voting Agreement controlled about 58% of total voting power as of April 8, 2026. Public Class A holders have economic exposure but limited practical governance leverage.

This is not automatically bad. Founder control can support long-duration product investment. It also deserves a discount when the company is pushing into highly regulated or politically sensitive areas such as event contracts, tokenized stocks, private-market access, credit cards, international crypto, and Trump Accounts.

StockAnalysis showed 900.5 million shares outstanding, 783.6 million float, 12.9% insider ownership, and 62.9% institutional ownership as of the May 14, 2026 page. That is a widely held mid/large-cap financial technology stock, not a neglected small-cap.

Short interest is not extreme. StockAnalysis showed 31.8 million shares short, equal to 3.5% of shares outstanding, 4.1% of float, and 1.1 days to cover. The short case is active in public debate, but this is not a crowded-short technical setup.

Insider activity is not a positive signal. Analyst sentiment is bullish but dispersed. StockAnalysis showed 23 analysts with a Buy consensus, average target of $105.87, median target of $96, low target of $48, and high target of $155. That range is the point. The market is not debating a small model tweak; it is debating whether Robinhood deserves a premium platform multiple or a cyclical brokerage/fintech discount.

Institutional ownership data is inconsistent across providers. ## Bull Case

The bull case starts with net deposits. Robinhood generated $68.1 billion of net deposits in FY2025 and $67.8 billion over the twelve months ended Q1 2026. April 2026 added another $6.0 billion. If those deposits are sticky, Robinhood is not just monetizing trading activity; it is becoming a primary financial relationship for a growing customer base.

The second pillar is product attach. A funded customer who only trades equities has limited value. A funded customer who uses Gold, maintains cash, borrows on margin, trades options, uses retirement, receives a card, participates in securities lending, buys crypto, trades event contracts, and eventually uses advisory/private markets is much more valuable. Robinhood has enough product breadth that ARPU can rise even without explosive funded-customer growth.

The third pillar is margin leverage. Robinhood is software-heavy and self-clearing, and a large part of incremental revenue should drop through if compliance, customer support, credit, and infrastructure costs remain controlled. FY2025 adjusted EBITDA margin was roughly 56% on reported adjusted EBITDA and net revenue. Even if that is cyclically high, it shows the earnings power in strong markets.

Prediction markets are the largest new call option. Event-contract revenue already reached $104 million in Q1 2026. If legal/regulatory outcomes stabilize and Rothera improves vertical integration, the business could become a meaningful, high-engagement revenue stream with Robinhood controlling distribution.

International crypto and brokerage expansion are additional options. Bitstamp, WonderFi, Europe stock tokens, Singapore, and Indonesia can expand the addressable market beyond U.S. retail brokerage. The risk is high, but the upside is real if Robinhood can replicate product velocity and brand trust in multiple markets.

Finally, capital allocation has improved. Robinhood is profitable, has a large cash balance, and is buying back stock. A profitable, high-growth retail finance platform with net cash and strong net deposits deserves a higher multiple than a structurally declining broker.

## Bear Case

The bear case is valuation versus durability.

In FY2025, transaction-based revenue was 59% of total revenue. Options, crypto, equities, and other transaction revenue are not annuity streams. They depend on volatility, retail trading intensity, asset prices, meme/crypto cycles, rebate rates, spreads, and product mix. In Q1 2026, crypto revenue fell 47% year over year. Robinhood still grew, which is positive, but the replacement growth came partly from event contracts, a product line with active legal and regulatory uncertainty.

PFOF remains a structural risk. Robinhood's own filings warn that market-structure changes, spread compression, reduced trading, liquidity-provider relationship changes, or regulation/bans of PFOF-like practices could reduce profitability and affect Robinhood more than competitors with less exposure. The 2024 SEC market-structure rules and 2026 compliance timeline deserve monitoring.

Liquidity-provider concentration is material. In 2025, Robinhood disclosed that market makers represented 55% of total net revenues, with Citadel Securities at 13% and Wintermute at 6%. That is not customer concentration in the normal SaaS sense, but it is economic plumbing concentration.

Net interest revenue may be cyclically flattered. FY2025 net interest revenue was $1.51 billion, 34% of total revenue, and Q1 2026 net interest revenue was $359 million, also 34%. If rates fall, cash balances migrate, margin borrowing slows, securities lending weakens, or customers require higher pass-through rates, this revenue stream can reset lower.

Card economics are early. Gold Card growth is impressive, but credit-card receivables and provisions are also growing. A fast-growing premium card can look attractive before cohorts season. Investors should watch delinquencies, charge-offs, provision builds, funding costs, reward economics, and whether card adoption actually improves broader account value.

Operational risk rises with product breadth. Robinhood has a history of outages and service disruptions, including prior trading and crypto disruptions. The product surface is now larger: 24-hour trading, crypto custody, event contracts, card, banking, AI tools, private markets, international markets, and government-linked account programs. The cost of a trust failure increases as the company becomes more central to customer financial lives.

Finally, the multiple is high. A business trading near 15x revenue and 37x earnings cannot merely be good. It must keep compounding at high rates, preserve high margins, avoid major regulatory impairment, and prove that new products are additive rather than expensive complexity.

## Base Case

The base case is that Robinhood is a better business than the old skeptic narrative but a less cheap stock than the current bull narrative.

In this case, Robinhood continues taking share among younger and mobile-native investors. Funded customers grow gradually. Total Platform Assets grow faster than funded customers because net deposits remain strong and users age into larger balances. Gold subscribers continue rising, but attach eventually slows. Margin grows but remains cyclical. Crypto recovers in bull markets and fades in quieter periods. Event contracts remain meaningful but face some legal and compliance friction. Advisory, retirement, card, RIA custody, and international products become real but not dominant.

Revenue compounds at around 10% for five years and 5% for the following five years. Adjusted EBITDA margins remain high, but after treating SBC, taxes, capex/software, and regulatory/working-capital needs as real costs, owner-earnings margins are closer to the low 30s than the headline adjusted EBITDA margin.

That is a good outcome. It is not enough for a large margin of safety at $76.75. In my DCF, that base case produces value around $47 per share. The current stock price requires either higher growth, higher owner-earnings margins, lower discount rates, more successful buybacks, or a higher terminal multiple.

### DCF Framework

Owner earnings formula:

`Owner earnings = revenue x adjusted EBITDA margin - cash taxes - capex/software capitalization - SBC treated as a real economic cost - incremental working/regulatory capital drag`

Scenario assumptions:

| Assumption | Bear | Base | Bull |
|---|---:|---:|---:|

### Reverse DCF

At $76.75 and 915 million diluted shares, the fully diluted equity value is about $70.2 billion. With $5.0 billion of net corporate cash, a 10% discount rate, a 20x terminal owner-earnings multiple, and a 32% after-tax owner-earnings margin, the market price implies roughly 15% annual revenue growth for a decade. That means revenue around $18 billion to $19 billion in 2036 and owner earnings around $6 billion.

Said differently: if growth slows to 8% in years 6-10, the current price requires roughly 20% annual growth in years 1-5, still with low-30s after-tax owner-earnings margins and no meaningful net dilution.

## Variant Perception

The clean bullish variant perception is that investors still anchor on Robinhood as a meme-era brokerage and underestimate its asset-gathering engine. In this view, the important metric is not one quarter of crypto revenue. It is the persistence of net deposits, Gold attach, margin balances, retirement assets, cash/deposit balances, advisory AUM, and event-contract engagement.

The clean bearish variant perception is that bulls are relabeling a cyclical trading business as a financial super-app before normalized economics are proven. Product launches are not a moat by themselves. The proof is incremental revenue, contribution margin, retention, CAC, compliance cost, credit performance, and customer trust through weak market conditions.

My view is closer to the middle. The asset-gathering engine is real and strategically valuable. The valuation already assumes that engine becomes durable enough to offset trading, crypto, rate, and regulatory cyclicality. That is possible, but it is not cheap.

## Conclusion

Robinhood is a real platform now. The company has moved beyond the simplistic "meme brokerage" label and has built a profitable, fast-moving retail financial-services ecosystem with strong net deposits and meaningful product attach.

The investment conclusion is less enthusiastic than the business-quality improvement. At $76.75, HOOD already prices in a lot of durable growth. My base case does not support the current price; the bull case does. That makes the stock a high-expectations compounder rather than a mispriced recovery story.

I would become more constructive if net deposits remain strong through a weaker market, event-contract legality stabilizes, Gold and card cohorts show attractive economics, non-transaction revenue becomes a larger share of revenue, and SBC/share count remain controlled. I would become more bearish if event contracts are restricted, crypto and options normalize lower, net interest revenue fades with rates, credit-card losses rise, or new initiatives keep lifting the cost base without clear monetization.

This is investment research, not personalized financial advice.
