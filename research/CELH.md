## 1. Thesis In One Page

### The Setup

Celsius is no longer a simple single-brand energy-drink challenger. In 2025 it bought Alani Nu, acquired U.S. and Canada rights to Rockstar from PepsiCo, moved Alani Nu into the Pepsi system, and became PepsiCo's U.S. strategic energy-category lead across CELSIUS, Alani Nu, and Rockstar. That makes the company more powerful with retailers and more complicated for common shareholders.

Q1 2026 looked spectacular on the surface: revenue grew 138% to $782.6 million, adjusted EBITDA grew 181% to $195.5 million, and the portfolio reached about 20.9% U.S. energy-drink dollar share. The debate is quality. Alani Nu contributed $368 million, Rockstar contributed about $67 million, and the legacy CELSIUS brand grew only about 6%. Q1 also benefited from Alani orders tied to the move into PepsiCo's system.

### Current View

CELH is a watchlist-quality consumer compounder with a live mispricing setup, but not a clean bargain. My base-case value is roughly $45 to $55 per share if Alani sell-through holds, gross margin recovers above 50%, and common shareholders participate after preferred-stock claims. The bear case is $20 to $25 if Q1 was heavy channel fill and margins stay structurally lower. The next proof point is not another headline revenue beat; it is sustained depletions, gross margin recovery, and cleaner cash conversion.

## 2. Company Story

### Origin And Evolution

Celsius built the original CELSIUS brand around fitness, zero sugar, and functional energy. The brand scaled quickly after the Pepsi distribution partnership expanded reach and shelf presence. The 2024-2025 slowdown shifted the narrative from hypergrowth insurgent to category participant with inventory and velocity questions.

The 2025 transactions changed the company. Alani Nu added a younger, more female-skewing lifestyle energy brand. Rockstar added a legacy mainstream brand in the U.S. and Canada. Pepsi became more central to strategy through distribution, preferred stock, board rights, and category execution.

### Why The Company Matters

Energy drinks remain one of the more attractive beverage categories because of frequent consumption, premium pricing, zero-sugar adoption, and expanding use cases. Celsius now has three brands with different consumer roles. That gives retailers a portfolio conversation rather than a one-brand growth pitch.

The company matters if it can become the Pepsi-system energy platform that complements or competes effectively with Monster, Red Bull, GHOST, C4, Prime, and private label. It fails if the acquisitions merely buy one-time shipment growth while weakening margin and balance-sheet quality.

## 3. Business Model

### How The Company Makes Money

Celsius sells branded energy drinks through distributors, retailers, club stores, e-commerce, and international partners. In the U.S. and Canada, Pepsi is the central route-to-market partner. Reported revenue depends on brand demand, shipments, retail execution, promotional allowances, distributor economics, and working capital.

| Revenue Stream | Description | Share / Size | Economics | Risk |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| CELSIUS | Flagship functional energy brand. | Q1 brand revenue grew about 6%. | Higher-quality legacy brand economics. | Maturity and share loss. |
| Alani Nu | Lifestyle energy brand acquired in 2025. | Q1 revenue $368M. | Large growth driver and new consumer cohort. | Channel-fill and acquisition-price risk. |
| Rockstar | U.S./Canada rights acquired from PepsiCo. | Q1 revenue about $67M. | Portfolio-control asset. | Brand decline and lower margin. |
| North America | Core market. | Q1 revenue $747M. | Scale and Pepsi DSD leverage. | Pepsi concentration. |
| International | Early growth option. | Q1 revenue $35M, +55% YoY. | Long runway if brand travels. | Small base and execution risk. |

### The Economic Engine

The engine is: build brand demand -> secure shelf/cooler space through Pepsi and retailers -> drive velocity -> expand portfolio share -> recover gross margin through scale and procurement -> convert earnings into FCF -> handle debt/preferred claims without diluting common upside.

The weak link is revenue quality. Q1 revenue can look excellent if distributors are loading inventory during a transition. The equity works if consumer takeaway, gross margin, and FCF confirm the shipments.

### What Bulls Believe

Bulls believe Celsius has become a scaled energy platform with three brands, Pepsi DSD execution, retailer relevance, Alani white space, and international optionality. Q1 shows that the portfolio can generate much higher earnings power than the market prices.

### What Bears Believe

Bears believe the original CELSIUS growth story slowed, forcing management to buy growth with Alani/Rockstar while accepting debt, dilution, Pepsi preferred, lower margins, and more working-capital complexity. Q1 was helped by transition orders and should not be annualized.

### What The Market May Be Missing

The positive variant view is that portfolio control is genuinely valuable and Alani is a durable second brand. The negative variant view is that common holders are now underwriting a more complicated beverage roll-up with less upside after Pepsi's claims.

## 10. Bull Case

If Alani Nu is a durable second core brand and not just a transition load-in, Celsius has a much bigger platform than the old one-brand story. A 20%+ portfolio share inside Pepsi's DSD system can improve retailer relevance, shelf space, and category influence. If gross margin recovers and FCF scales, today's valuation is too low.

## 11. Bear Case

If Q1 was inflated by channel fill and the legacy CELSIUS brand is maturing, the stock can be a value trap. Alani and Rockstar may lower gross margin, increase trade spend, and require more Pepsi dependency. Preferred stock and debt make the common less clean.

## 12. Base Case

The base case assumes the portfolio is real but Q1 overstates run-rate quality. Revenue grows high single to low double digits from a larger FY2026 base, gross margin recovers to 51%-52%, and FCF margin moves toward 12%. That supports a value in the mid-$40s to mid-$50s if the preferred claim is handled conservatively.

CELH is a better portfolio than it was two years ago, but a messier common equity. Alani Nu and Pepsi's system can create a real second leg, while Rockstar gives the company more category control. Q1 showed the upside. It also showed why investors should not simply annualize headline revenue or adjusted EBITDA.

My stance is watch / cautiously constructive. At $30.12, the stock is interesting if Alani demand is durable and gross margin recovers. The next report should focus less on headline growth and more on brand-level sell-through, gross margin, promotional allowances, Pepsi concentration, and preferred-stock economics.
