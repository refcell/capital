## Read

- The bear case is not a total collapse. It assumes regulatory intervention reduces growth to 4% and compresses margins, but LOPE remains profitable and generates cash. Even in the bear case, the stock at ~$115 after 10 years represents a modest negative CAGR of -9.6% from current levels — the regulatory hit hurts the multiple more than the bottom line. This is plausible if gainsharing restrictions are enacted and LOPE must transition to a lower-margin model.
- The base case is the most likely outcome. LOPE continues growing at 7%, maintaining 27%+ operating margins while regulatory uncertainty fails to materialize into concrete legislation. The 10.7-11.3% price CAGR combined with share repurchases creates attractive total returns. This requires the market to eventually recognize that the fundamentals have not broken and reprice the stock to a normal 23-26x P/E multiple.
- The bull case requires both regulatory normalization and above-trend revenue growth of 10%. The 14.7% price CAGR is achievable if LOPE proves to be a durable quality compounder with expanding margins. This scenario represents the full upside if the regulatory story resolves positively and online education demand remains strong.
- Stock prices in each case work backward from explicit year-10 terminal P/E anchors (12x / 23-24x / 28x), exactly as requested. The declining P/E in later years of the high-growth scenarios reflects earnings growth outpacing price appreciation in the compounding process.
- Revenue growth and earnings trajectories from FY2027 onward are extrapolated beyond the visible Q1 FY26 data and should be treated as projections, not forecasts. Q1 FY26 actual EPS of $2.86 and revenue of $309M provide the latest anchor point.

## Key Assumptions

- Revenue growth projections are extrapolated from a consistent 7% YoY trajectory observed from FY2022 through FY2025/TTM. The base case preserves this rate; bear reduces to 4%; bull accelerates to 10%.
- Operating margins of ~27-28% in the base case are anchored in current TTM results of 27.6%. The bear case compresses to ~22-24% due to regulatory adaptation costs and loss of higher-margin gainsharing income.
- Share count of 25.5M at the 5-10 year horizon assumes continued aggressive buybacks reducing shares from the current 27.6M by approximately 8%. Buyback pace of ~$250-300M annually is consistent with FY25 results.
- The workbook uses FCF-based exit multiples (12x/16x/20x) for the Valuation and Scenarios sheets. The projections translate these to P/E-based anchors for consistency with Value Line-style formatting, resulting in similar terminal price targets.
- Net cash adjustment of ~$311M adds to equity value in all cases. This is stable because LOPE has no debt and the cash position should remain meaningful even as buybacks consume excess cash.
- FCF yield = estimated free cash flow / market cap for that year. Higher FCF yields correspond to more attractive valuations.
- The `research/LOPE.md` report contributes the qualitative framework. The conclusion is "watch" with a $180-200 probability-weighted fair value range, 35% implied upside, and regulatory risk as the primary overhang.
