## Read

- The bear case assumes that the Midera spin-off removes a material revenue engine (approximately 30-35% of current revenue) and that the remaining CFEG platform cannot grow meaningfully, capping out at sub-2% annual revenue growth. The buyback program is constrained by post-spin credit tightening, so per-share earnings growth is minimal. Operating margins compress from 18% toward 15-16% as tariff costs and labor inflation hit the foodservice equipment mix harder without the food processing offset. The terminal P/E of 12x is deliberately low — it reflects a "stalled specialty machinery company with no growth story and negative tangible book value" multiple. The bear CAGR of -4.0% is a modest erosion from current levels over 10 years.

- The base case uses analyst consensus through FY2027, then assumes 2-3% annual revenue growth as the CFEG platform benefits from natural QSR capex replacement cycles, modest share gains, and continued international presence. The key driver is share count reduction: starting from 44.8M current shares, continued buybacks at 2-3% annually compound per-share earnings growth of 5-8% even with modest revenue growth. Operating margins remain stable at 18-19% because the foodservice equipment mix is inherently higher-margin than food processing. FCF yield improves modestly as a percentage of MC because the multiple is reasonable and cash generation is strong. The terminal P/E of 16x is conservative for a company currently trading at 17.7x forward — it assumes no re-rating. The base CAGR of +1.8% means the investment slightly outperforms inflation but does not compound dramatically.

- The bull case assumes that the spin-off unlocks sum-of-the-parts value that the market has not yet recognized. CFEG re-rates as a clean, focused foodservice equipment platform with no legacy food processing drag, driving multiple expansion to 18-20x. Revenue growth of 3.5-7% requires share gains in the QSR segment, new IoT-connected kitchen equipment sales, and continued international expansion. The buyback program accelerates because CFEG's standalone FCF profile is stronger than expected post-spin. The terminal P/E of 20x is justified only if the market reclassifies Middleby from "stagnant industrial" to "growing platform" — which requires revenue growth acceleration as the primary evidence. The bull CAGR of +5.5% is aggressive but achievable if the re-rating narrative takes hold and buybacks compound the earnings base.

## Key Assumptions

- Analyst consensus from Yahoo Finance provides FY2026-FY2027 anchors (11 analysts for both years). FY2028 and beyond are extrapolated beyond the visible public estimate horizon.
- FY2028-FY2036 scenario rows are model extrapolations based on the workbook scenario framework, not visible analyst estimates.
- Operating margin assumptions: bear 15-16%, base 18-19%, bull 19-20%. These bracket the current 17.9% TTM operating margin.
- Net income uses projected operating income less estimated interest ($90-100M/year) and a 24% effective tax rate.
- FCF yield uses projected FCF divided by projected market cap, assuming FCF margin improves modestly toward 8-9.5% as a percentage of revenue due to operating leverage at the top end.
- Share count is modeled at 44.8M (current implied shares) with moderate decline through buybacks in the base/bull cases. Bear case assumes buyback slowdown.
- Stock price works backward from explicit year-10 terminal P/E anchors: 12.0x bear, 16.0x base, and 20.0x bull. Interim prices use a constant CAGR path from the current $173.36 price to the year-10 terminal value.
- P/E at each horizon is calculated as projected stock price divided by projected per-share EPS (net income divided by estimated diluted shares).
