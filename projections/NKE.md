## Read

- The base case is mostly a margin-recovery case, not a revenue-growth case. FY2027 revenue is pinned near visible consensus, and the 10-year outcome depends on EBIT margin normalizing from the low-single-digit trough toward the low teens.
- The bear case is a value-trap path: Nike stabilizes revenue but cannot restore product heat, China, Direct, Converse, or FCF conversion enough to justify even today's depressed multiple.
- The bull case requires a cleaner Win Now turnaround, sport-led product recovery, healthier wholesale sell-through, and a return toward historical premium-brand economics without assuming Nike regains its old growth multiple.

## Key Assumptions

- FY2027 revenue and EPS use Seeking Alpha's visible low/consensus/high estimates as the near-term bear/base/bull anchor. FY2028 uses MarketScreener and StockAnalysis consensus as a cross-check, then applies explicit scenario spreads because public low/high were not visible.
- FY2029, FY2031, and FY2036 are extrapolated from the online estimate path, `models/[2026-06-01] NIKE Model.xlsx`, and `research/NKE.md`; they are not visible analyst low/high estimates.
- Operating income is GAAP-like operating income derived as revenue times scenario operating margin. Bear reaches only 8.0% EBIT margin by FY2036, base reaches 13.0%, and bull reaches 16.0%.
- Net income is calculated from diluted EPS and projected diluted shares. Bear uses roughly flat shares, base assumes modest buybacks, and bull assumes stronger buyback resumption as FCF recovers.
- FCF yield is projected FCF per share divided by projected stock price. Scenario FCF margins move from depressed FY2026 levels toward 6.3% bear, 8.6% base, and 10.8% bull by FY2036.
- Terminal P/E anchors are 14.0x bear, 18.0x base, and 23.0x bull. These are selected below Nike's historical premium multiple in the base case because current evidence still shows weak gross margin, weak Direct, weak Converse, and compressed FCF.
- Interim stock prices are walked back from each case's FY2036 terminal price to the current $45.32 price on a constant annualized price-return path.
- Revenue growth is YoY for consecutive displayed years. For FY2031 and FY2036, it is the annualized growth rate from the prior displayed fiscal year.
