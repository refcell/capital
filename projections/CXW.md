## Read

- The bear case uses analyst low estimates through FY2027, then assumes revenue growth essentially stalls below 2% annually as ICE detention volume normalizes and facility contracts age without renewal uplift. Operating margin compresses to 8-9% as maintenance capex rises on aging facilities and debt service absorbs a growing share of operating income. The terminal P/E of 14x reflects an ESG-discounted, politically-impaired multiple. The bear CAGR is mildly negative (-5.5%) — this is a deteriorating-compound scenario where the stock slowly loses value as the capex cycle proves structural rather than cyclical.

- The base case uses analyst consensus for FY2026-FY2027, then extrapolates modest growth of 3% annually through FY2036 as the business transitions from high-revenue expansion (ICE-driven) to steady-state operations. The 10-year terminal P/E of 18x reflects normalization — the business is not cheap in perpetuity but is not overpriced either. Net income grows via share count reduction from sustained buybacks, which the FCF supports when the capex cycle ends. The base CAGR is near zero (+0.1%) — this position essentially tracks inflation but is unlikely to compound meaningfully.

- The bull case uses analyst high estimates for FY2027, then assumes sustained mid-to-high single-digit revenue growth as the company commissions additional facilities, wins new contracts, and benefits from secular growth in detention capacity demand. Margins expand toward 12% as scale benefits offset higher capex. The terminal P/E of 22x for the year-10 anchor is generous — it requires multiple expansion that only happens if the political/ESG headwinds fade or the market becomes indifferent to ESG exclusion. The bull CAGR is +2.9%, which is respectable but not extraordinary — CXW's debt and political overhang limit the ceiling even in a favorable scenario.

## Key Assumptions

- Analyst consensus from Yahoo Finance provides FY2026-FY2027 anchors (5 analysts for FY2026, 3 for FY2027). FY2028 and beyond are extrapolated beyond the visible public estimate horizon.
- FY2029-FY2036 scenario rows are model extrapolations based on the workbook scenario framework, not visible analyst estimates.
- Operating margin assumptions: bear 8-9%, base 10-11%, bull 12-14%. These bracket the current TTM 10.4% operating margin.
- Net income assumptions use the operating margins above and the historical effective tax rate of ~25.9%, with interest expense modeled at ~$88M/year based on the blended cost of existing debt.
- FCF yield uses projected FCF divided by projected market capitalization, assuming FCF margin normalizes toward 4-8% as the capex cycle ends. The current 1.1% FCF margin is a trough value.
- Share count is modeled at 98.89M (current implied shares) with moderate decline through buybacks in each case. No new dilution is assumed.
- Stock price works backward from explicit year-10 terminal P/E anchors: 14.0x bear, 18.0x base, and 22.0x bull. Interim prices use a constant CAGR path from the current $29.73 price to the year-10 terminal value.
- P/E at each horizon is calculated as projected stock price divided by projected EPS (net income / diluted shares at ~94-99M range depending on buyback pace).
