## Read

- The bear case is severe. A 10-year CAGR of -17.6% means the acquisition fails to create value and the market ultimately prices VECO as a slow-growth equipment company with depressed margins. The 14x terminal P/E is a contraction from the current embedded 26x, reflecting the market losing patience with protracted integration. This scenario becomes more likely if FY26 FY27 results come in below consensus.

- The base case is the most likely path. The acquisition integrates over 2–3 years with near-term friction but eventual margin recovery. Revenue grows at 15% driven by combined product portfolio and cross-selling. The 11.0% price CAGR over 10 years is compelling but requires current investors to hold through near-term volatility. From the $20 base, this is a 680% total return; from $80, it's more modest.

- The bull case requires execution at the high end of every analyst estimate, a favorable semiconductor capex cycle, and Veeco capturing disproportionate share in the fastest-growing equipment categories. The 13.4% CAGR over 10 years is achievable if the synergies compound meaningfully. Revenue at 22% CAGR beyond FY27 is aggressive extrapolation.

- Stock prices in each case work backward from explicit year-10 terminal P/E anchors (14x / 22x / 26x) with FCF multiple cross-checks. The declining P/E trajectory in the bull case reflects earnings growth outpacing price appreciation in the compounding process.

- Revenue growth and earnings trajectories from FY2028 onward are extrapolated beyond the visible FY26-FY27 public estimates and should be treated as projections, not forecasts.

## Key Assumptions

- Revenue growth projections for FY2027 are anchored in the Yahoo Finance consensus estimates ($763.8M FY26, $1.0B FY27). Years beyond FY27 are extrapolated at the scenario-specific CAGRs.
- Operating margin recovery from the current depressed 4.6% TTM level toward 10%+ is assumed in the base and bull cases. This is the single most important assumption — without it, the earnings path does not materialize.
- Share count normalizes from 61.03M toward ~56M over the 5–10 year horizon, assuming the company resumes buybacks once integration stabilizes and FCF exceeds $150M annually.
- The workbook uses FCF-based exit multiples (14x/18x/22x) with the WACC of 11.37% as the discount framework. The projections translate this to P/E-based anchors for Value Line-style formatting.
- Net debt adjustment of $99M subtracts from equity value in all cases. This is stable assuming no additional deal-related leverage.
- FY27 EPS consensus of $3.05 was revised upward significantly from $2.10 just 30 days ago, indicating high uncertainty and potential for further revision either direction.
- FCF yield = estimated free cash flow / market cap for that year. Higher yields in later years reflect improving profitability relative to the stock price.
