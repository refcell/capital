## Read

- The bear case is now an analyst-anchored downside case, not the old local stress case. It uses visible FY2027-FY2028 low estimates, keeps FY2029 near visible online consensus, and then assumes slower compounding and a below-base terminal multiple.
- The base case says the reset is useful: revenue compounds to about $3.4B over 10 years, normalized net margin reaches 15%, and the stock earns a high-single-digit to low-double-digit return from today's lower multiple.
- The bull case needs rhode, Naturium, international, and core e.l.f. Cosmetics to remain a real multi-brand platform. The terminal value is powerful, but the falling FY2036 FCF yield shows how much valuation support comes from a premium terminal P/E.

## Key Assumptions

- FY2027 and FY2028 revenue and EPS use company guidance and visible public analyst low/average/high estimates as the near-term scenario anchor. FY2029 uses visible online consensus as a cross-check, while FY2031 and FY2036 are extrapolated from the online estimate path, the local workbook, and `research/ELF.md` scenario debate.
- Operating income is normalized operating income for scenario comparison. FY2027 and FY2028 net income use adjusted/normalized EPS because GAAP earnings are distorted by rhode acquisition accounting and contingent-consideration fair-value changes.
- Bear assumes FY2027-FY2028 analyst-low estimates, FY2029 near visible online consensus revenue, mid-single-digit growth thereafter, FCF margin reaching 12.5%, annual share dilution around 1.5%, and a 16.0x terminal P/E.
- Base assumes high-single-digit revenue growth through FY2031, mid-single-digit growth thereafter, FCF margin reaching 15.5%, annual share dilution around 1%, and a 20.0x terminal P/E.
- Bull assumes rhode and international keep growth elevated through FY2031, FCF margin reaches 17%, annual share dilution around 0.5%, and a 26.0x terminal P/E.
- Revenue growth is YoY for consecutive displayed years. For FY2031 and FY2036, it is the annualized growth rate from the prior displayed fiscal year.
- FCF yield is projected FCF per share divided by projected stock price. P/E is projected stock price divided by projected normalized diluted EPS.
- Interim stock prices are walked back from each case's FY2036 terminal price to the current $61.15 price on a constant annualized price-return path.
