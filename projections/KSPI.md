## Read

- The bear case is not a collapse case. It assumes the 2026 tax/reserve/funding headwinds persist, Turkiye stays around breakeven longer, and the stock remains structurally cheap at a 5.5x terminal P/E.
- The base case follows visible KZT consensus through FY2028, then assumes Kaspi keeps compounding but remains a Kazakhstan/Turkiye financial platform valued at a single-digit terminal P/E.
- The bull case requires Hepsiburada engagement to move materially toward Kaspi.kz levels, fintech/e-commerce economics to converge in Turkiye, and geopolitical/regulatory risk to stop compressing the multiple.

## Key Assumptions

- FY2026 and FY2027 use visible online low/average/high EPS and revenue estimates where available. FY2028 uses visible MarketScreener consensus as the main anchor because low/high estimates were not visible.
- The literal FY2027 Seeking Alpha revenue low is below the FY2026 KZT consensus path and conflicts with EPS growth, so the bear case uses a moderated KZT revenue path rather than copying that single low value mechanically.
- Revenue, operating income, and net income are shown in KZT billions. Stock price and P/E use USD per ADS.
- Per-ADS valuation uses approximately 190.03 million ADS/common shares and a constant KZT487/USD conversion, which aligns the visible USD EPS estimates with KZT revenue consensus.
- For KSPI, the FCF yield column uses normalized distributable earnings yield, calculated as projected net income per ADS divided by projected stock price. Reported operating cash flow is not a clean owner-earnings measure because the business includes fintech, lending, deposits, and marketplace working-capital movements.
- Bear/base/bull terminal P/E anchors are 5.5x, 8.0x, and 10.0x FY2035 EPS. The low base-case multiple reflects Kazakhstan/Turkiye country risk, bank/fintech regulation, currency risk, and minority-investor discount.
- Revenue growth is YoY for consecutive displayed years. For FY2030 and FY2035, it is the annualized growth rate from the prior displayed fiscal year.
- Interim stock prices are walked back from each case's FY2035 terminal price to the current $80.56 price on a constant annualized price-return path.
