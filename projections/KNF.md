## Read

- The bear case is severe. A 10-year CAGR of -17.1% means the acquisition fails to create value and the company ultimately trades as a stagnant, over-leveraged regional player. The 14x terminal P/E is a contraction from the current 27x embedded forward P/E, reflecting the market losing patience with protracted integration. This scenario becomes more likely if FY26-FY27 results come in below consensus or if capex stays above $300M for multiple consecutive quarters. From the $58.72 52-week low, the bear target would be near the prior support zone.

- The base case is the most likely path. Capex normalizes by FY2027, revenue grows at 7% driven by the combined platform's geographic diversification and the infrastructure tailwind. The 10.2% price CAGR over 10 years is solid but requires current investors to hold through FY2026 integration uncertainty. From the current price of $92.90, this is modest upside — the stock is essentially fairly valued on the base case. The WACC of 6.54% from the workbook supports this as the discount rate against which the scenarios are weighed.

- The bull case requires execution at the high end of every analyst estimate, a favorable infrastructure spending cycle, successful acquisition integration, and KNF capturing disproportionate share in the fastest-growing regional aggregates markets. The 13.5% CAGR over 10 years is achievable if synergies compound meaningfully and deleveraging occurs as expected. Revenue at 11% CAGR beyond FY27 is aggressive extrapolation — it represents roughly 3% from the current base plus 8% from the post-acquisition platform that hasn't yet been in place through a full year.

- Stock prices in each case work backward from explicit year-10 terminal P/E anchors (14x / 22x / 26x) with FCF multiple cross-checks consistent with the workbook model. The declining P/E trajectory in each case reflects earnings growth outpacing price appreciation in the compounding process.

- Revenue growth and earnings trajectories from FY2028 onward are extrapolated beyond the visible FY26-FY27 public estimates and should be treated as projections, not forecasts.

## Key Assumptions

- Revenue growth projections for FY2027 are anchored in the Yahoo Finance consensus estimates ($3.44B FY26, $3.63B FY27). Years beyond FY27 are extrapolated at the scenario-specific CAGRs.
- Operating margin recovery from the current depressed 8.8% TTM level toward 9-10% (base) or 12%+ (bull) is the single most important assumption. This requires capex normalization by FY2027-Q1.
- Share count remains stable at ~56.75M through the projection period. No buybacks are assumed until FCF exceeds $300M annually.
- The workbook uses FCF-based exit multiples (10x / 14x / 18x) with the WACC of 6.54% as the discount framework. The projections translate this to P/E-based anchors for Value Line-style formatting.
- Net debt adjustment moves from ~$1.47B in FY2026 to $0.3-0.9B in later years as capex normalizes and OCF funds debt repayment. The base case assumes $0.7B net debt by year 5.
- Interest expense of $87M TTM is the loaded level. As debt declines, interest expense falls, compressing the gap between operating income and net income.
- FY27 EPS consensus of $4.10 is from only 1 analyst. The range ($3.87-$4.75) reflects substantial uncertainty.
- Capex normalization is the linchpin: if capex returns to $170-180M, FCF recovers to ~$300M per year by FY2027, supporting the base and bull projections. If capex stays above $300M, only the bear case materializes.
- FCF yield = estimated free cash flow / revenue for that year. Higher yields in later years reflect improving profitability as capex declines.
