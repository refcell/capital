## Read

The bear case is a multiple-compression scenario, not an earnings-implosion scenario. Even in the bear case, ICE generates $3.9 billion in net income at year 10 — essentially flat from current levels. Revenue grows 3% annually, which is slow but not negative. The wealth destruction comes from P/E declining from the current 17.8x to 14.0x as the market loses confidence in the quality of the franchise. This would require: sustained mortgage technology underperformance, regulatory action on exchange fees, and failed buyback execution. The year-10 price of $96.60 represents a 22% further decline from the current $123.11.

The base case is the most likely outcome. ICE grows revenue at 7% — driven by the exchange core (6-8% organic), data subscription expansion (8-10%), and mortgage technology contribution (4-6%). The multiple remains stable at 17.8-18.0x, reflecting the current market's view that ICE is a quality company with some integration uncertainty. Earnings compound at ~7.5% CAGR, slightly ahead of revenue due to modest operating leverage. The year-10 price of $239.80 represents 6.7% annualized price CAGR from current levels — a respectable but not spectacular return for a financial infrastructure compounder.

The bull case requires BlackKnight to prove highly accretive. This is plausible: mortgage servicing revenue is counter-cyclical (delinquencies drive servicing demand), and BlackKnight's AVM data business scales independently of origination volumes. If the mortgage segment stabilizes, management accelerates buybacks, and exchange fee revenue remains intact, ICE could trade at a 22x premium comparable to CME and S&P Global. The 11.8% price CAGR in the bull case would make ICE one of the best-compounding financial infrastructure stocks in the sector. This requires that the market loses its "acquisition complexity discount" conviction — which happens organically as quarters of stable mortgage margin data accumulate.

Stock prices in each case work backward from explicit year-10 terminal P/E anchors (14x / 18x / 22x), consistent with the workbook model's FCF yield and EV/EBITDA valuation framework. The P/E columns are cross-checks that reflect gradual multiple convergence from ICE's current 17.8x P/E toward the terminal anchor. FCF yield columns show why the base case is compelling: even in year 10, ICE generates a 5.0% FCF yield on enterprise value — well above the WACC of approximately 7.9%, confirming that the business earns above its cost of capital in every scenario.

## Key Assumptions

- Revenue growth projections from FY2028 onward are extrapolated beyond the visible 2-year analyst forecast window. Analyst consensus estimates FY2027 revenue at $11.53B, which appears materially below the FY25 actual of $12.64B — this discrepancy suggests analysts may be using a pro-forma basis that excludes or restates BlackKnight. The projection tables above use extrapolation from the actual TTM revenue of $13.077B. If analyst estimates are the correct basis (i.e., BlackKnight revenue will step down from TTM levels), then all projection figures overstate revenue by approximately 15-20%.

- EPS growth projections for Years 1-2 are anchored to Yahoo Finance analyst consensus ($8.14 FY26, $8.81 FY27). EPS growth beyond Year 2 is derived from revenue growth assumptions divided by declining share counts. The share count assumption of 567M (current) declining to 530M (base) or 510M (bull) by year 10 reflects buyback programs of $1-2B annually, which ICE has already begun executing ($1.7B TTM repurchase).

- Terminal P/E anchors (14x / 18x / 22x) are consistent with peer valuations in the exchange/data category: CME trades at ~24x, S&P Global at ~22x EV/EBITDA, NDAQ at ~14x, and ICE historically at 17-20x. The bear case of 14x would align ICE with NDAQ (lower-quality exchange with more trading-dependent revenue). The bull case of 22x would align ICE with S&P Global (the quality premium).

- Net margin assumptions: bear 24%, base ~28%, bull 32%. Current TTM net margin of 30.1% is inflated by a one-time $414M "other income" benefit. Normalized FY2025 net margin was closer to 26.2%. The base case of 28% reflects modest improvement from normalized levels due to operating leverage and buyback-driven EPS growth.

- The "other income" TTM benefit of $414M is treated as non-recurring in these projections. FY2025 "other income" was only $31M on $4.3B pretax income — a negligible line item. This one-time boost means current P/E of 17.9x understates the true trailing multiple on a normalized basis (normalized P/E would be closer to 19-20x).

- FCF yield figures assume FCF grows roughly in line with revenue (7-8% CAGR). Current FCF TTM is $4.24B. Capex is assumed to remain at 5-6% of revenue (consistent with $778M TTM capex on $13.1B revenue). If ICE enters a significant technology investment cycle (e.g., major exchange platform refresh or BlackKnight technology upgrade), capex could temporarily spike and FCF yields would be lower in affected years.
