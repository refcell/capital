## Read

- The bear case is a return to the trough. Garden segment continues structural decline at 2% per year (moderating the -3-5% rate already visible), pet segment stalls as private label gains share, and the multiple compresses. Even in the bear case, the stock retains value — $23.50 after 10 years is a -17.1% CAGR but CENTA still generates real free cash flow and owns valuable brands. The $28.03 target from the workbook is more aggressive than this table, reflecting the possibility of deeper margin compression.

- The base case is the most likely outcome. Revenue stabilizes near $3.2B as the pet segment's structural growth offsets garden decline. Operating margins expand to 9%+ as the company benefits from deleveraging completion, and FCF consistently exceeds $280M annually. The 17.1% price CAGR over 10 years is driven by a combination of margin expansion, share count reduction through buybacks, and modest multiple normalization. This requires no dramatic change in the business — just the continuation of current trends.

- The bull case requires the pet segment to become the dominant revenue driver, growing at 5-8% annually. This is plausible given the $147B pet care market and Nylabone's brand loyalty, but it is extrapolation beyond the visible analyst consensus (which projects revenue decline). The 3.0% revenue CAGR assumes the company successfully transitions from a "garden + pet" company to a "pet + garden" company in terms of growth dynamics. The 38.2% price CAGR would make CENTA one of the better compounding consumer stocks in the market.

- Stock prices in each case work backward from explicit year-10 terminal FCF multiple anchors (9x / 11x / 14x), consistent with the workbook model's FCF-based valuation approach. The P/E columns show implied valuation at each horizon as a cross-check.

- Revenue and earnings trajectories from FY2027 onward are extrapolated beyond the visible FY2026/FY2027 analyst estimates ($3.05B / $2.95B revenue, $2.97 / $3.09 EPS). The Q2 FY26 actual EPS of $1.29 and revenue of $906.15M, plus the Q2 beat pattern (+17.3% surprise), support upward pressure on earnings estimates.
