## Read

- The bear case is a credit-cycle / derating case, not a revenue-collapse case. It follows analyst-low FY2027-FY2028 revenue and EPS, then assumes growth remains healthy but the market values SOFI closer to a cyclical consumer lender.
- The base case lines up with the local research note's 2030 value range: FY2031 stock price is about $29, with the 10-year return coming from earnings compounding rather than multiple expansion.
- The bull case needs deposits, LPB, Financial Services, and underwriting quality to scale together. It assumes SOFI becomes a high-ROE digital financial platform, but the 20.0x terminal P/E still embeds bank/credit-cycle risk.

## Key Assumptions

- FY2027 and FY2028 use visible BusinessQuant low/consensus/high revenue and EPS estimates as the primary bear/base/bull anchor. StockAnalysis and MarketScreener are used as consensus cross-checks, especially because MarketScreener's FY2028 revenue consensus is lower.
- FY2029, FY2031, and FY2036 are extrapolated from the online estimate path, Q1 2026 run-rate, company guidance, and `research/SOFI.md`; they are not visible analyst low/high estimates.
- Operating income is normalized GAAP-style operating income / EBIT for scenario comparison. Net income is normalized earnings attributable to common shareholders.
- For SOFI, the displayed FCF Yield column is normalized earnings yield, calculated as projected EPS divided by projected stock price. Reported free cash flow is not a clean owner-earnings measure because SoFi is a lender/bank with deposits, loan growth, fair-value accounting, securitizations, and capital requirements.
- Per-share math uses approximate diluted/common share counts of 1.30B in FY2027, 1.31B in FY2028, 1.32B in FY2029, 1.33B-1.34B in FY2031, and 1.34B-1.38B in FY2036 depending on scenario.
- Revenue growth is YoY for consecutive displayed years. For FY2031 and FY2036, it is the annualized growth rate from the prior displayed fiscal year.
- Terminal P/E anchors are 12.0x bear, 16.0x base, and 20.0x bull. The range is below pure software multiples because lending, credit, funding costs, regulatory capital, and fair-value marks remain central to the business.
- Interim stock prices are walked back from each case's FY2036 terminal price to the current $16.58 price on a constant annualized price-return path.
