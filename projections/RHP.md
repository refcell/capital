## Read

- The bear case is not a collapse case. It assumes RHP remains a respectable lodging REIT, but same-store convention demand cools, acquisition benefits prove less accretive per share than hoped, and the market eventually values the stock closer to a plain quality cyclical than a scarcity asset.
- The base case says the current quote is near fair. Revenue keeps compounding, operating margins stay solid, and per-share earnings rise, but the stock mostly earns a low-single-digit price CAGR because today’s valuation already capitalizes much of the business quality.
- The bull case needs more than healthy hotel demand. It needs Desert Ridge and the broader resort platform to drive cleaner per-share compounding, while the market keeps assigning a premium multiple to a scarce convention-resort operator.
- These stock-price CAGR figures exclude dividends. Because RHP currently yields about 3.9%, total return would be higher than the price-only CAGR if the dividend remains intact and grows moderately.

## Key Assumptions

- Public estimate visibility is limited. The live free StockAnalysis page shows FY2026 low/average/high adjusted EPS and flat revenue at $2.80B, plus FY2027 aggregate revenue of $2.91B and EPS of $4.96. It does not show public FY2027 low/high or full FY2028 detail without gating, so everything from FY2028 onward is extrapolated.
- The local workbook contributes the medium-term scenario structure. Its Scenarios sheet uses 3.5% / 6.0% / 8.5% FY2026-FY2031 revenue CAGR assumptions and 15x / 17x / 19x exit FCF multiples, which I used as an operating-trajectory cross-check rather than the direct pricing method because `projections/README.md` requires stock prices to work backward from year-10 terminal P/E anchors.
- `research/RHP.md` contributes the qualitative debate and medium-term valuation framing: bear around $101, base around $136, and bull around $166 depending on whether RHP turns good resort operations into durable per-share FFO growth. The base and bear 5-year rows are intentionally close to that local framing.
- Operating income is a normalized consolidated operating-income path, not a direct copied Street estimate. Net income is normalized adjusted earnings consistent with the public adjusted EPS framing on StockAnalysis, which is why P/E is shown even though P/FFO remains the better REIT lens.
- For RHP, the displayed FCF Yield column is normalized forward FFO/share divided by projected stock price, used as an owner-earnings yield proxy. Reported free cash flow is too distorted by maintenance capex, renovation timing, and acquisition activity to serve as the clean primary valuation anchor for a lodging REIT.
- Share-count assumptions are modestly dilutive in bear and base and roughly stable in bull, reflecting the fact that acquisition-led growth can create absolute EBITDA without creating equal per-share value.
- Revenue growth is YoY for consecutive displayed years. For FY2031 and FY2036, it is the annualized revenue CAGR from the prior displayed fiscal year.
- Interim stock prices are walked back from each case’s FY2036 terminal price to the current $122.81 price on a constant annualized price-return path, exactly as requested in `projections/README.md`.
