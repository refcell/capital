## Read

- The bear case now uses visible analyst low estimates only for FY2027-FY2028, then assumes Celsius remains a good but more normal beverage compounder instead of fading into low-single-digit growth.
- The base case is run-rate adjusted: it assumes Q1 2026 was not just channel fill, Alani remains a real second platform, and revenue can compound well above ordinary consensus through 2031.
- The bull case follows the more aggressive model-driven path already flagged in `research/CELH.md`, with FY2027 revenue around $5.6B and FY2028 revenue around $8.7B, then assumes international and portfolio expansion remain exceptional.

## Key Assumptions

- BusinessQuant's visible low/consensus/high revenue estimates remain the ordinary street anchor, but the base and bull cases are now adjusted upward because Q1 2026 revenue, Alani contribution, and the VCP Scanner directional model make ordinary consensus look too conservative if the sell-through is real.
- Bear uses analyst low estimates through FY2028, then extrapolates. Base and bull deliberately depart from consensus beginning in FY2027.
- FY2029 through FY2036 are extrapolated because visible public low/consensus/high estimate tables were not available for the full 10-year horizon.
- FY2036 is extrapolated beyond the local research note's explicit scenario horizon. The 10-year case assumes bear remains a solid compounder, base becomes a scaled energy platform, and bull becomes a global category winner.
- Revenue growth is YoY for consecutive displayed years. For FY2031 and FY2036, it is the annualized YoY growth rate from the prior displayed fiscal year.
- FCF yield uses projected FCF divided by projected common market capitalization. P/E is calculated as projected stock price divided by projected EPS using 255.64M shares.
- Stock price works backward from a year-10 terminal P/E anchor: 14.0x bear, 24.0x base, and 28.0x bull. Interim stock prices use a constant CAGR path from the current $29.18 price to the year-10 terminal value.
- The table does not model Pepsi preferred conversion mechanics directly; it embeds that risk through lower common-equity P/E assumptions in the medium-term stock-price path.
