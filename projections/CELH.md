## Read

- The bear case is channel-fill fade: Alani slows after the Pepsi transition, gross margin recovery stalls, and common-equity value remains pressured by Pepsi preferred claims.
- The base case assumes Alani Nu is real demand, but Q1 2026 was not a clean run-rate quarter. Revenue grows from the current FY2027 consensus base toward roughly $5.6B in FY2031, while operating margin recovers to about 21%.
- The bull case requires sustained Alani sell-through, international growth, gross margin recovery into the mid-50s, and cleaner FCF conversion.

## Key Assumptions

- FY2027 base revenue and EPS are anchored to current consensus. Bear and bull cases flex that anchor for lower or higher Alani/Rockstar sell-through and margin recovery.
- The FY2031 scenario anchors match the local CELH research note: bear around $4.7B revenue and 8% FCF margin, base around $5.6B revenue and 12% to 13% FCF margin, and bull around $7.0B revenue and 16% FCF margin.
- FY2036 is extrapolated beyond the local research note's explicit scenario horizon. The 10-year case assumes bear growth fades materially, base growth stays mid-single digit, and bull growth remains category-leading.
- FCF yield uses projected FCF divided by projected common market capitalization. P/E is calculated as projected stock price divided by projected EPS using 255.64M shares.
- Stock price is a scenario common-equity output, not the analyst average price target.
- The table does not model Pepsi preferred conversion mechanics directly; it embeds that risk through lower common-equity P/E assumptions in the medium-term stock-price path.
