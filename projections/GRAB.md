## Key Assumptions

- FY2027 revenue uses visible public low/consensus/high estimates as the primary bear/base/bull anchor. FY2028 uses MarketScreener consensus plus calibrated scenario spreads because public low/high estimates were not visible.
- FY2029, FY2031, and FY2036 are extrapolated from the online estimate path, company guidance, Q1 2026 run-rate, long-run margin logic, and the new CAGR-spread heuristic; they are not visible analyst low/high estimates.
- The standard bear/base/bull return spread is calibrated to 8 percentage points around base. Visible FY2027 revenue and EPS dispersion alone would imply a smaller spread, but FY2028 low/high visibility is thin and 12-month price-target dispersion is meaningfully wider.
- Operating income is IFRS / GAAP-style operating income. FY2027-FY2028 base operating income is tied to visible MarketScreener / StockAnalysis EBIT estimates, then extrapolated by case.
- Net income is normalized net income attributable to shareholders. EPS estimates differ by provider and by GAAP versus non-GAAP presentation, so the tables use normalized EPS paths and disclose this in the source notes.
- FCF yield uses adjusted free cash flow per share divided by projected stock price. Grab's adjusted free cash flow is a non-IFRS measure, and financial-services balance-sheet growth can make reported cash flow noisy.
- Bear assumes revenue growth fades to high single digits, operating margin reaches 22%, adjusted FCF margin settles near 12.5%, share count rises to 4.25B, FY2036 EPS is $0.50, and terminal P/E is 14.25x.
- Base assumes revenue reaches $15.0B in FY2036, operating margin reaches 27%, adjusted FCF margin reaches 18%, share count falls to 3.75B, FY2036 EPS is $0.95, and terminal P/E is 15.3x.
- Bull assumes Grab compounds revenue to $19.5B by FY2036, operating margin reaches 34%, adjusted FCF margin reaches 23%, share count falls to 3.45B, FY2036 EPS is $1.55, and terminal P/E is 18.3x.
- Revenue growth is YoY for consecutive displayed years. For FY2031 and FY2036, it is the annualized growth rate from the prior displayed fiscal year.
- Interim stock prices are walked back from each case's FY2036 terminal price to the current $3.30 price on a constant annualized price-return path.
