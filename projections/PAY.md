## Read

- The bear case is a consolidation-complete / growth normalization scenario. It follows the analyst-low FY2027 revenue, then assumes the mid-market payments opportunity has largely played out. The 18.0x terminal P/E is a mature infrastructure multiple.
- The base case follows analyst consensus for FY2027 ($1.69B revenue) and assumes continued but gradually decelerating growth. The terminal P/E of 20.0x is reasonable for a fintech with proven operating leverage and a net cash balance sheet.
- The bull case requires management to issue formal guidance, initiate capital returns, and validate the multi-year compounding trajectory. The 24.0x terminal P/E is aggressive but achievable if analyst coverage expands to 10+ names and the company demonstrates 15%+ sustained revenue growth for three consecutive years.
- A key asymmetry in favor of the long side: the company sits on $339 million in cash (15% of enterprise value) with no debt. Even a conservative $50M annual buyback at current prices would retire ~2.4M shares (1.9% of float), providing meaningful per-share mechanical support.
- All revenue and earnings figures for years beyond FY2028 are extrapolated — no public estimate sources extend that far. The extrapolation follows linear growth deceleration from the analyst consensus anchors.
