## Bear Projection

Terminal P/E anchor: 14x FY2036 EPS. Revenue CAGR 5.0%, adjusted FCF margin 2.5%, exit multiple 12x FCF on terminal revenue. The bear case assumes the acquisition was overpriced and the integration is protracted and painful. Operating margins compress and never recover to double digits — the acquired platform has structurally worse economics than the organic base. FY27 EPS comes in at the low end of consensus (~$2.03), and subsequent earnings fail to accelerate. Revenue growth stalls at roughly 5% as the combined platform struggles to realize synergies. The stock falls as the market loses patience with the recovery narrative and re-rates toward a single-digit multiple on a slow-growth, low-margin platform. At 14x terminal P/E on declining per-share earnings, the implied price is deeply below current.

Revenue growth at 5% from FY29 onward is extrapolated beyond visible public estimates (which only go to FY28). This represents a modest compounder scenario where the combined entity survives but does not generate excess returns.

## Read

- **The bear case is severe.** A 10-year CAGR of -11.8% means the stock falls to approximately $15 per share as the market re-rates StepStone from a growth story to a slow-commoditized platform. The bear case becomes more likely if FY27 Q2 earnings at Aug 6 show operating margins below 5% and revenue continues to decline. The 14x terminal P/E reflects a multiple contraction from the current embedded 16.3x, representing the market's patience wearing thin.

- **The base case is the most likely path.** The acquisition integrates over 2-3 years with near-term friction but eventual margin recovery. Revenue grows at 12% driven by combined product portfolio, AUM compounding, and new institutional mandates. The 8.3% price CAGR over 10 years is modest but reflects the current price being fairly valued for a recovery story that already has 16x baked in. From $40.66, the price doesn't explode because the multiple does not expand — it holds at 16-17x through the recovery.

- **The bull case requires execution at the high end of every analyst estimate plus additional catalysts.** The PitchBook data platform becomes a significant revenue driver, cross-selling accelerates, and the market re-rates toward the 24x multiple of the largest asset managers. The 11.2% price CAGR over 10 years is compelling but requires both earnings growth and multiple expansion. Revenue at 18% from year 3 onward is aggressive extrapolation.

- **Stock prices in each case work backward from explicit year-10 terminal P/E anchors** (14x / 20x / 24x) with FCF multiple cross-checks. The P/E trajectories compress in early years (earnings recovery pushes P/E down even as price holds) then expand toward the terminal anchor.

- **Revenue growth and earnings trajectories from FY2029 onward are extrapolated** beyond the visible FY27-FY28 public estimates and should be treated as projections, not forecasts. The Q2 FY26 revenue data of $312.8M (down 14.1% YoY) is the first evidence point for which direction the trend is moving.

## Key Assumptions

- Revenue growth projections for FY27 ($1.57B) and FY28 ($1.77B) are anchored in Yahoo Finance consensus estimates. Years beyond FY28 are extrapolated at the scenario-specific CAGRs.
- Operating margin recovery from the current -51.3% TTM level toward 12-15% is assumed in the base and bull cases. This is the single most important assumption — without it, the earnings path does not materialize.
- Share count stabilizes near 80M (from current 80.7M), modestly declining as buybacks resume once FCF exceeds $150M annually.
- The workbook uses FCF-based exit multiples (12x/18x/22x) with WACC of 8.10% as the discount framework. The projections translate this to P/E-based anchors (14x/20x/24x) for Value Line-style formatting.
- Net cash adjustment of ~$185M adds to equity value in all cases. Debt of $1.31B and cash of $1.12B are stable assuming no additional deal-related leverage.
- FY27 EPS consensus of $2.49 has been under revision pressure — down from $2.53 (90 days ago). FY28 EPS of $3.29 also down from $3.37. Negative revision trend is a material risk.
- FCF yield = estimated free cash flow / implied market cap for that year. Higher yields in later years reflect improving profitability relative to the stock price.
- Tax rate assumption: ~21% effective rate for forward projections. TTM tax rate is unreliable (positive provision on net loss).
- Dividend of $1.67/share (4.04% yield at $40.66) provides downside support in all scenarios.
