## Read

- The bear case is not a revenue-collapse case. ZETA still compounds revenue, but the market keeps valuing it like a controversial adtech / data business, so most of the operating progress is absorbed by lower terminal multiple and dilution.
- The base case assumes Zeta clears the Marigold integration and data-trust hurdles well enough to keep growing high teens to low 20s near term, then settles into low-teens long-run growth with FCF margins moving toward the high teens.
- The bull case requires the data cloud, Athena, Marigold cross-sell, and direct platform mix to work together. It also assumes the controversy discount fades enough for ZETA to recover a software-like terminal multiple.

## Key Assumptions

- FY2027 revenue and EPS use visible public low/consensus/high estimates as the primary bear/base/bull anchor. FY2028 uses the visible estimate range as a downside check, but base and bull are adjusted toward management's $2.3B+ 2028 revenue target and other visible market forecasts because the BusinessQuant FY2028 consensus sits below that target.
- FY2029, FY2031, and FY2036 are extrapolated from the online estimate path, management's 2028 target, Q1 2026 run-rate, and `research/ZETA.md`; they are not visible analyst low/high estimates.
- The operating-income column is labeled as adjusted EBITDA because ZETA's guidance, local research note, and visible market model are built around adjusted EBITDA and FCF. GAAP operating income is less useful here because acquisition amortization, SBC, and other adjustments distort comparability.
- Normalized net income is based on adjusted/normalized EPS. Approximate diluted share counts are 255M/253M/251M in FY2027, 258M/255M/252M in FY2028, and 280M/265M/258M in FY2036 for bear/base/bull, respectively.
- Bear assumes revenue slows after the Marigold step-up, adjusted EBITDA margin reaches 25% by FY2036, FCF margin ends near 12.5%, and terminal P/E is 12.0x.
- Base assumes revenue reaches $6.8B in FY2036, adjusted EBITDA margin reaches 28%, FCF margin reaches 18.5%, buybacks mostly offset SBC dilution, and terminal P/E is 18.0x.
- Bull assumes ZETA becomes a durable AI/data marketing platform, revenue reaches $10.0B in FY2036, adjusted EBITDA margin reaches 32%, FCF margin reaches 22%, buybacks offset dilution, and terminal P/E is 24.0x.
- Revenue growth is YoY for consecutive displayed years. For FY2031 and FY2036, it is the annualized growth rate from the prior displayed fiscal year.
- FCF yield is projected FCF per share divided by projected stock price. P/E is projected stock price divided by projected normalized diluted EPS.
- Interim stock prices are walked back from each case's FY2036 terminal price to the current $20.21 price on a constant annualized price-return path.
