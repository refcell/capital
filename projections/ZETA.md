## Read

- The updated bear case is still below consensus, but it is no longer a near-zero-return stress case. Visible analyst dispersion supports a weaker growth/margin path and lower terminal multiple, not a scenario where ZETA compounds fundamentals for a decade while the share price barely moves.
- The base case says the controversy discount narrows gradually if Zeta clears Marigold integration, data-trust, and cash-conversion tests. FY2027-FY2028 stay close to visible consensus and management's 2028 target, then long-run growth fades into low double digits.
- The bull case remains driven by Zeta proving that its data cloud, Athena, direct platform mix, and enterprise expansion are durable enough to deserve a software-like terminal multiple. The upside gap is now intentionally close to the downside gap around base.

## Key Assumptions

- FY2027 revenue and EPS use visible public low/consensus/high estimates as the primary bear/base/bull anchor. FY2028 uses the visible estimate range as a downside check, but base and bull are adjusted toward management's $2.3B+ 2028 revenue target and other visible market forecasts because the BusinessQuant FY2028 consensus sits below that target.
- FY2029, FY2031, and FY2036 are extrapolated from the online estimate path, management's 2028 target, Q1 2026 run-rate, `research/ZETA.md`, and the new CAGR-spread heuristic; they are not visible analyst low/high estimates.
- The standard bear/base/bull return spread is calibrated to roughly 9 percentage points around base. Visible FY2027 EPS dispersion and 12-month price-target dispersion justify a wider spread than revenue dispersion alone.
- The operating-income column is labeled as adjusted EBITDA because ZETA's guidance, local research note, and visible market model are built around adjusted EBITDA and FCF. GAAP operating income is less useful here because acquisition amortization, SBC, and other adjustments distort comparability.
- Normalized net income is based on adjusted/normalized EPS. Approximate diluted share counts are 255M/253M/251M in FY2027, 260M/255M/252M in FY2028, and 286M/265M/258M in FY2036 for bear/base/bull, respectively.
- Bear assumes revenue reaches $5.2B in FY2036, adjusted EBITDA margin reaches 25%, FCF margin reaches 14.5%, share count rises to 286M, FY2036 EPS is $2.50, and terminal P/E is 15.9x.
- Base assumes revenue reaches $7.2B in FY2036, adjusted EBITDA margin reaches 29%, FCF margin reaches 19%, buybacks mostly offset SBC dilution, FY2036 EPS is $5.10, and terminal P/E is 17.5x.
- Bull assumes ZETA becomes a durable AI/data marketing platform, revenue reaches $10.0B in FY2036, adjusted EBITDA margin reaches 32%, FCF margin reaches 22%, buybacks offset dilution, FY2036 EPS is $7.85, and terminal P/E is 24.0x.
- Revenue growth is YoY for consecutive displayed years. For FY2031 and FY2036, it is the annualized growth rate from the prior displayed fiscal year.
- FCF yield is projected FCF per share divided by projected stock price. P/E is projected stock price divided by projected normalized diluted EPS.
- Interim stock prices are walked back from each case's FY2036 terminal price to the current $20.21 price on a constant annualized price-return path.
