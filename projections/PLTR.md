## Read

- The bear case is a multiple-compression case, not a revenue-collapse case. Revenue compounds at ~11% to $26B by FY2036, and operating margins hold at ~34%, but the terminal P/E compresses from the current ~131x (TTM) / ~79x (forward) toward 25x — a mature-software multiple that the market applies when the AIP differentiation is viewed as a feature of larger platforms rather than a standalone moat. Even in bear, Palantir generates $6.7B of net income — the losses come from valuation, not business failure.
- The base case lines up with the local research note's scenarios: FY2027 anchors at Yahoo Finance consensus $11.2B revenue and $2.08 EPS, then growth decelerates from ~30% to ~22% to ~13% over the decade. Operating margin stays in the 33-36% range. Terminal P/E of 35x reflects sustained AI-platform premium above mature norms. The 10-year CAGR of +17.0% is driven by earnings compounding, not multiple expansion.
- The bull case requires AIP to become the definitive AI deployment layer — analogous to how Salesforce became the CRM standard or Oracle the database standard. Revenue doubles to $82B by FY2036 with 34% operating margins, and the market sustains 45x P/E on the basis that the installed base creates unassailable switching costs. This is achievable but demands near-flawless execution and zero competitive disruption.

## Key Assumptions

- FY2027 revenue and EPS anchors are taken directly from Yahoo Finance visible consensus estimates ($11.2B revenue, $2.08 EPS base; $10.32B / $1.59 bear; $14.36B / $2.72 bull). These are the only years with explicit low/avg/high analyst estimates on the public page. FY2028+ are extrapolated.
- Operating margins are set at ~36% for FY2027 (consistent with current GAAP margin trajectory), then settle toward 34-35% in bear, 33-36% in base, and 32-36% in bull as the law of large numbers and competitive pressure introduce modest operating-expense acceleration.
- Net income margin incorporates a forward tax rate of approximately 17% (between the current TTM effective rate of 1.3% and the statutory 21%, reflecting expected normalization as R&D credits and NOLs are exhausted and the company scales past $10B+ in annual revenue).
- Per-share math uses approximate diluted share counts growing at ~3-4% annually from the current ~2,570M (2,690M in FY2027, 2,830M in FY2028, 3,000M in FY2029, 3,400M in FY2031, 4,000M in FY2036), consistent with historical SBC-driven dilution of ~4.5% annually.
- FCF Yield column is normalized earnings yield (projected net income per share / projected stock price) as a proxy, because Palantir's FCF approximates operating cash flow (capex is trivial) and the earnings yield captures the return an owner receives at the given price. Reported FCF margin of ~51.5% TTM is used as the quality benchmark.
- Revenue growth is YoY for consecutive displayed years. For FY2031 and FY2036, it is the annualized growth rate from the prior displayed fiscal year.
- Terminal P/E anchors are 25.0x bear (mature-software norms), 35.0x base (AI-platform premium), and 45.0x bull (definitive infrastructure layer). These are below current levels (~79x forward, ~131x TTM) because multiple compression is the mechanism in bear and the normalization path in base/bull.
- Interim stock prices are walked back from each case's FY2036 terminal price to the current $113.50 price on a constant annualized price-return path, per projections/README.md.
- FY2029, FY2031, and FY2036 rows are extrapolated beyond visible public estimates. They are anchored to the research note (`research/PLTR.md`) scenario model and the 6-sheet workbook (`models/[2026-06-24] Palantir Model.xlsx`) terminal-year math.
