## Read

The bear case is a cycle-peak scenario. The insurance industry is currently in a hard cycle where pricing power is strong, combined ratios are below 100%, and surplus compounds rapidly. If this cycle turns within the next 3-5 years, THG's earnings will compress from current levels — potentially to $470-$510M annually (from $721M TTM), and the P/B multiple will compress from 2.08x toward 1.6x. Even in the bear case, THG retains significant value: $195/share after 10 years represents a $140B in retained earnings on book value, and the company still earns $430M annually — well above break-even. The 10-year price CAGR of -13.3% is the worst outcome but not a capital destruction scenario.

The base case is the most likely outcome. Revenue stabilizes around 4% annual growth — consistent with analyst consensus — as the hard cycle supports pricing but not dramatic expansion. The key insight is that BVPS growth of 8% for 5 years followed by normalization to 5-6% is the pattern most insurance cycles follow: hard years compound book value rapidly, then the cycle turns and accumulation slows. The 24.2% price CAGR over 10 years is driven by a combination of ROE-driven book value compounding and continued buyback acceleration. This requires no dramatic shifts in the business — just continuation of current underwriting discipline.

The bull case requires the hard cycle to persist for 5+ more years — longer than the typical 3-5 year cycle window — and for THG's specialty focus to generate outsized market share gains relative to larger competitors. The 62.8% price CAGR would make THG one of the best-compounding insurance stocks in the sector. This requires that catastrophe losses remain below average for the full decade, that the specialty segment continues to outpace the broader insurance market, and that management maintains aggressive buyback discipline through years of price appreciation. The P/B expansion to 2.4x is within the historical range for high-ROE insurers (CINF trades at 2.2x, some specialty carriers reach 2.5x), but requires sustained 20%+ ROE that is rare outside of the best cycle years.

Stock prices in each case work backward from explicit year-10 terminal P/B anchors (1.6x / 2.0x / 2.4x), consistent with the workbook model's P/B + ROE valuation framework. The P/E columns are cross-checks that reflect the fact that THG is currently trading at depressed P/E multiples (10.7x trailing) relative to its earnings — as book value compounds, these multiples will naturally decline unless earnings grow faster than price.

The bull case sustains 20-23% through persistent hard cycle conditions.

- Insurance cycle timing: The current hard cycle in US P&C insurance began around 2020-2021. Historical cycles last 3-5 years from inception. This suggests the current cycle could persist through 2024-2026 (base case window) or extend to 2026-2028 (bull case). The bear case assumes a cycle turn by 2028-2029.
